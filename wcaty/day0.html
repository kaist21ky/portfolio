Bayesian Uno

Players

  • One Player, facing the Dealer.
  • If multiple Players, the Dealer deals a face down card for each during Setup, and the Players take turns during Play.

Cards

  • A deck of four 1s, 2s, 3s, 4s, 5s, and 6s.
  • Or any other similar set of six contiguous numbers.
  • The Player knows what set of numbers are in the deck.

Setup

  1. The Dealer shuffles the deck.
  2. The Dealer draws a card, looks at it, and places it face down, hidden from and in front of the Player.
    This is the card the Player must guess the number on to win during Play.
  3. If multiple Players, repeat the above step once for each.

Players

  • The Player may say, "I need a hint."
    1. If they do, the Dealer draws a card, looks at it, and says if this new card's number is greater than, less than, or equal to the card to win.
    2. This new card is left face down on the table.
    3. If there are no cards left in the deck, the Dealer picks up and shuffles all cards left on the table this way to create a new deck.
      The card to win is left on the table.
  • Or the Player may say, "That card is a ...".
    1. If they do, the Dealer turns the card to win face up.
    2. If the Player's guess was correct, they keep the card to win to represent one point.
      Then the Dealer deals a new face down card to win as they did during Setup.
    3. If the Player's guess was wrong, the dealer keeps the card to win to represent one point to the House.
      Then the Dealer deals a new face down card to win as they did during Setup.

Victory

  • First Player to 3 points wins
  • If the House reaches 2 points times the number of Players, then the House wins instead
